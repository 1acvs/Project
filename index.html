The Unrivaled Champion

Tom Brady’s unprecedented seven Super Bowl victories cement him as the winningest player in NFL history — a record that spans two franchises and nearly two decades of dominance.

From his first triumph as a 24-year-old underdog in Super Bowl XXXVI to his seventh at age 43 with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers, Brady’s championship legacy eclipses every franchise in the league and doubles the total of every other quarterback to ever play the game.

From Montana's candy-cool comeback to Brady's 28-3 resurrection, these are the locker-room stories that turned rings into legend. Quick hits of heart-stopping drives, goal-line stands, and halftime heroics that still echo in every huddle.

The Birth of a Dynasty

On January 15, 1967, the NFL’s Packers faced the AFL’s Chiefs in a showdown that proved the merger’s worth it.Lombardi’s Packers proved NFL superiority, gave the game its first dynasty moment, and stamped the word “Super Bowl” into American culture forever

Location: Los Angeles Memorial Coliseum

Date: 15 January 1967

Scoreboard: Packers 35, Chiefs 10

Riggins 43-Blast

In the warm Pasadena twilight, John Riggins turned a 4th-and-1 hand-off into a 43-yard, tackle-breaking touchdown gallop that flipped a deficit into a Redskins lead they would never surrender. Riggins earned MVP, and the Redskins captured their first Lombardi Trophy, 27-17.

Location: Rose Bowl Pasadena, California

Date: 30 January 1983

Scoreboard: Washington Redskins 27, Miami Dolphins 17

One Yard Short The Greatest Show on Turf

Steve McNair’s Titans answered with a 13-play, 64-yard march to the St. Louis 10, but on the final play Mike Jones wrapped up Kevin Dyson at the 1-yard line: “One Yard Short.” The tackle sealed the Rams win.

Location: Georgia Dome Atlanta, Georgia

Date: 30 January 2000

Scoreboard: St. Louis Rams 23, Tennessee Titans 16

Wide Right—One Giant Upset

With eight seconds left, Scott Norwood lined up from 47 yards, the Bills trailing 20-19. The ball left his foot looking true, then drifted—just barely—outside the right upright. In that heartbeat, Buffalo’s first championship hopes vanished.

Location: Tampa Stadium Tampa, Florida

Date: 27 January 1991

Scoreboard: New York Giants 20, Buffalo Bills 19

The Interception

Down four with 26 seconds left, the dynastic New England Patriots watched Seattle march to the 1-yard line, poised to steal the title. Rookie CB Malcolm Butler intercepts Russell Wilson at the goal line with 20 seconds left to preserve the win.

The Comeback That Broke the Scoreboard

28-3 late in the third, then the earth tilts. Tom Brady engineers five straight touchdown drives—capped by two 2-point conversions and James White’s 1-yard plunge in overtime completes the first-ever OT in Super-Bowl history.

Date: 5 Feburary 2017

Scoreboard: Patriots 34, Falcons 28

Philly Special

The game turned on a daring 4th-down “Philly Special” trick play that saw Foles catch a touchdown pass from tight end Trey Burton, and it ended with a Brandon Graham strip-sack of Brady in the final two minutes—cementing the underdog Eagles as champions in the highest-scoring Super Bowl in history at the time.
